The Mystery on the Summit

On the summit of Turkey Hill in Chester, Connecticut, ancient grooves carved into bedrock tell a story we're still learning to read. Are they marks left by the first people of this land, patiently sharpening the tools they used to build their world? Or are they scars from the death of the ice age itself, carved by rivers of meltwater under a mile of ice?

Two Competing Theories. One Investigation.

Hypothesis A: The Polissoir - A sacred worksite where Native Americans sharpened and polished stone tools for generations. U-shaped grooves suggest patient, deliberate human craftsmanship.

Hypothesis B: The Glacial Channel - A geological marvel carved 15,000 years ago by high-pressure meltwater under continental ice. V-shaped grooves would indicate the cutting force of an ancient river.

This is where we broadcast from. A place where three timelines converge: Deep Time, Ancient History, and the Modern Era. Every episode begins at the firepit on the summit, exploring technology, AI, human creativity, and the stories stones can tell.
